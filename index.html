About

I am a medical AI researcher with an MSc in Mechatronics Engineering and a background in biomedical engineering. My work sits at the intersection of deep learning and clinical medicine, with a focus on building reliable, interpretable models for biomedical signal processing and medical imaging.

My research focuses on AI for biomedical signal processing and medical imaging, with particular emphasis on multimodal learning for cardiology and oncology applications. Current interests include ECG-based risk prediction, tumor detection and segmentation from MRI/CT, explainable AI, uncertainty-aware clinical decision support, and multimodal fusion of imaging, physiological, and clinical data.

Beyond research, I have hands-on industry experience developing electronics for medical devices — including ECG acquisition hardware — and have taught applied AI workshops to clinical and research staff. I am a member of the Iran National Elites Foundation.
